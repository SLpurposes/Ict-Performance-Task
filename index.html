Strands in Senior High School Philippines

Introduction

In the Philippines, high school education spans grades 7 through 12 under the K-12 curriculum. A key feature of this system is the introduction of academic strands in the last two years of high school, allowing students to specialize in specific areas that align with their skills, interests, and career aspirations.

Importance

The use of strands in high school plays a critical role in the education system, providing numerous benefits:

  • Career Preparation: Students can focus on subjects relevant to their desired careers, gaining foundational knowledge and skills.
  • Flexibility and Personalization: The strands allow students to tailor their education to their interests, promoting engagement and motivation.
  • Bridging Education and Employment: Specialized training in specific fields helps students transition into the workforce with the right skill set.
  • Facilitates Higher Education: Students who plan to continue their studies can choose strands that align with college programs, easing the transition to higher education.

Use

In the Philippine high school system, students can choose from various strands as part of one of four main tracks:

  • Academic Track:
    • Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM)
    • Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)
    • Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS)
    • General Academic Strand (GAS)
  • Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) Track: Offers practical skills and training for vocational careers like electronics, culinary arts, automotive technology, and IT.
  • Sports Track: Designed for students with a passion for sports, focusing on physical education and sports science.
  • Arts and Design Track: For those interested in visual and performing arts.

General Academic Strand (GAS)

In the context of education, the term "gas strand" refers to the General Academic Strand. It is a strand offered in senior high school that takes a generalist approach in preparing students for college. The General Academic Strand is designed for students who are still undecided about which career path they want to pursue in the future. It covers various subjects and topics, including humanities, social sciences, organization, and management. The General Academic Strand allows students to explore different subjects and disciplines, giving them exposure to a variety of career options. It provides a well-rounded educational experience and allows students to make a more informed decision regarding their undergraduate program and future career path. Some of the possible career paths for students who graduate from the General Academic Strand include educators, businessmen, lawyers, professors, psychologists, doctors, writers, journalists, multimedia designers, interior designers, and social workers. After completing the General Academic Strand, students can enroll in various undergraduate courses such as Bachelor of Science in Multimedia Arts and Sciences, Bachelor of Fine Arts in Digital Cinema, Bachelor of Science in Interior Design, and Bachelor of Science in Physical Education. It is important to note that the General Academic Strand is just one of the options available in senior high school, and students should choose a strand that aligns with their interests and future goals.

Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM)

The ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management) strand is an academic strand offered in senior high school in the Philippines. It focuses on preparing students for careers in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and management. The ABM strand covers subjects such as marketing, accounting, business ethics, and organization and management. It equips students with the knowledge and skills necessary to understand the business world and prepares them for further studies in business-related courses in college. After completing the ABM strand, students can pursue careers as accountants, entrepreneurs, business managers, marketing professionals, financial analysts, and more.

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)

The STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) strand is an academic strand offered in senior high school in the Philippines. It focuses on preparing students for careers in the fields of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. The STEM strand covers subjects such as physics, chemistry, biology, mathematics, and computer science. It emphasizes critical thinking, problem-solving, and hands-on experience. After completing the STEM strand, students can pursue college courses in engineering, computer science, mathematics, physics, and other STEM-related fields. The STEM strand prepares students for careers in areas such as engineering, technology, research, and data analysis.

Technical Vocational Livelihood (TVL)

TVL stands for Technical-Vocational-Livelihood track. Through the TVL senior high school track, students can gain the skills and knowledge they need to seek for employment straight after senior high school whether that's in the Philippines or overseas. Jobs you can take up after pursuing TVL! Industrial Arts Track

1. Architect

Are you into design or building form and structure? If you choose industrial arts, you will learn more about the effort and technicalities behind the creation of buildings and other infrastructures—exactly what you need to be an architect! Architects map out designs for structures and oversee how they are constructed; they are the brains behind the buildings you see today. This career path requires a lot of patience and meticulousness, but it is highly rewarding when you have a role in the establishment of well-built homes, businesses, and more.

2. Engineer

If you want to be a changemaker who makes the lives of people more comfortable, this career is for you! Engineering requires technical skills and knowledge to address issues surrounding electricity, transportation, and machinery; thus, this is best accompanied with an engineering course to identify your specialization. But even with just TVL’s specialized subjects, you can be motivated to trailblaze with a new innovation or system!

3. Development Researcher

While taking the agri-fishery track, you will learn about agriculture, animal healthcare, food processing, and organic farming. You will likely develop a strong inclination towards community, which is perfect for the position of development researcher! This position allows you to analyze farming practices, land development, animal cultivation, and more. Being a development researcher means being able to balance your advocacy alongside career!

4. Agronomist

Research is still integral to agronomy, but it is more specific to crops! For all the plantitas and plantitos who want to take their love of nature to the next level, this one is for you. Argonomists are the liaison between farmers who grow the crops and other parties. They provide insights using their botanical knowledge to any stakeholders that ask, be it governments, third-party businesses, or even non-governmental organizations!
